Making Sense of the Numbers

Understanding Your Home Loan Options

Whether you are a first home buyer or a seasoned property investor, knowing your numbers is the first step to securing the right finance. Use our guides below to understand how lenders assess your profile.

Mortgage Repayments & Rates

Your monthly repayment is determined by your loan amount, the interest rate, and the loan term (typically 30 years). Choosing to pay fortnightly or weekly can often help you pay off your loan faster by sneaking in extra payments each year. Always compare rates, but remember that the lowest rate doesn't always equal the best loan structure.

Borrowing Power in Melbourne

Lenders calculate your borrowing capacity by assessing your income against your expenses and existing debts. They also apply a "buffer" rate (usually 3% above the actual interest rate) to ensure you can still afford repayments if rates rise. Reducing credit card limits and unnecessary expenses can significantly increase how much you can borrow.

Stamp Duty & Government Fees

Stamp duty is a state government tax calculated on the purchase price of your property. If you are a first home buyer purchasing an established or new home, you may be eligible for significant stamp duty concessions or full exemptions, depending on the property value and your state's current legislation.

The Power of an Offset Account

An offset account is a transaction account linked directly to your home loan. The balance in this account is offset daily against your loan balance when calculating interest. For example, if you have a $500,000 loan and $50,000 in your offset account, you only pay interest on $450,000. This feature can save you tens of thousands of dollars over the life of your mortgage.
