Asset finance options

Choose a structure that suits how you use the asset.

There is more than one way to finance a car or piece of equipment. We explain the differences in plain language so you can choose confidently with your accountant.

Chattel mortgage

Popular for business vehicles and equipment where your ABN entity owns the asset from day one.

Potential tax and GST benefits, with flexible balloon options to keep repayments lower.

Lease & hire purchase

Structures where the lender retains ownership or title conditions during the term.

Can suit certain tax positions or where you want a cleaner balance sheet presentation.

Low‑doc options

For established business owners where full financials are not readily available.

Lenders may rely on ABN age, asset type, property ownership or bank statements instead of full tax returns.

A simple, fast asset finance process.

  1. Quick discovery – we confirm the asset, purchase price, how it will be used and who will own it (personal, business, trust).
  2. Lender and structure selection – we compare options across our panel to find a structure that balances repayments, flexibility and tax treatment (with your accountant’s input).
  3. Approval & settlement – we coordinate with the dealer or supplier, manage documentation and keep you updated through approval, invoice payment and delivery.

We recommend you obtain tax advice on which structure is most appropriate for your circumstances.

Illustrative scenario

A self‑employed electrician needed a new ute and tools package worth $78k. We arranged a chattel mortgage with a manageable balloon, using low‑doc assessment based on ABN age, bank statements and property ownership. The structure kept monthly repayments comfortable while allowing his accountant to optimise the tax treatment.

This is a general example only and not advice. Asset finance suitability and pricing depend on your individual profile and lender criteria.
