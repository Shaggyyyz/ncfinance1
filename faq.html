General & Broker Services

A bank can only offer you their own specific loan products. If their policy doesn't suit you, or their rates are uncompetitive, they won't tell you to go to their competitor. As mortgage brokers, Nest Capital Finance acts as an intermediary, providing access to over 60 lenders, including major banks, second-tier lenders, and specialist funds. We compare rates, fees, and policies to find the solution tailored to your specific needs, manage all the paperwork, and advocate on your behalf.

In most residential lending scenarios, there is no charge to you for our service in most residential lending scenarios. We are paid an upfront commission and a small trailing commission by the lender upon settlement of your loan. This commission is disclosed fully in our credit proposal document. Importantly, the interest rate you receive is the same (or often heavily discounted) as if you went direct, as lenders do not charge you extra for using a broker.

Best Interests Duty (BID) is a legal requirement in Australia that applies to mortgage brokers (but crucially, not to bank branch staff). It mandates that brokers must act in the best interests of the consumer when providing credit assistance. If there is a conflict of interest, we must prioritize your interests above our own. This provides you with unmatched protection and transparency.

First Home Buyers & Preparation

Ideally, a 20% deposit avoids Lenders Mortgage Insurance (LMI) and gives you immediate equity. However, most lenders will allow you to buy with a 5% to 10% deposit (plus purchasing costs) if you pay LMI. Alternatively, if you qualify for the Federal Government's Home Guarantee Scheme, you can purchase with a 5% deposit and pay $0 in LMI. If your parents are willing to act as Guarantors, you can borrow 100% of the property value with no cash deposit at all.

LMI is a one-off insurance premium paid by the borrower, but it exists to protect the lender in the event you default on your loan and the property is sold at a loss. It is generally required if your Loan-to-Value Ratio (LVR) exceeds 80%. While it adds to your loan cost, it allows buyers to enter the property market years earlier than if they had to save a full 20% deposit.

A family guarantee allows a family member (usually parents) to use the equity in their own home as additional security for your loan. This means you can borrow up to 100% of the purchase price plus costs (like stamp duty) without paying LMI. Once the new property increases in value or you pay down the debt to an 80% LVR, the guarantor can be released from the loan.

Pre-approval is a formal indication from a lender that they are willing to lend you a specific amount, subject to you finding a suitable property and a satisfactory valuation. It is essential before attending auctions. Pre-approval validity periods vary by lender — typically ranging from 90 to 180 days. Confirm current timeframes with us when applying. We can usually "refresh" it easily by providing your latest payslips.

Refinancing, Debt & Features

We recommend a home loan review every 12 to 18 months. You should actively look to refinance if: 1) Your interest rate starts with a higher number than what banks are advertising to new customers, 2) Your fixed-rate period is expiring (avoid the "loyalty tax" revert rate), 3) Your property has grown in value and you want to extract equity to invest or renovate, or 4) You need to consolidate personal debts.

An Offset Account is a separate, everyday transaction account linked to your loan. Every dollar inside it "offsets" your loan balance for interest calculations. A Redraw Facility is a feature of the loan itself that allows you to withdraw extra repayments you have already made. For tax purposes (especially if you plan to turn your home into an investment property later), an offset account is far superior because withdrawing from an offset does not constitute a "new borrowing" in the eyes of the ATO, preserving the tax deductibility of the original loan.

Sometimes yes, sometimes no. Some lenders offer cashback incentives to win your business (amounts vary and are subject to change). However, if the new interest rate is higher than alternatives on the market, the extra interest you pay over 3 to 4 years will wipe out the upfront cash. We run a "Net Benefit Calculation" for you that factors in discharge fees, new setup fees, the cashback amount, and the interest rate to show you exactly which loan leaves you wealthier over a 3 to 5 year horizon.

Debt consolidation significantly improves your monthly cash flow because you move high-interest debt (e.g., a 15% car loan) to a much lower home loan rate (e.g., 6%). However, the danger is stretching a 5-year car loan over a 30-year mortgage term, which costs more in long-term interest. We recommend isolating the consolidated debt into a separate "loan split" and continuing to pay it off aggressively.

Investment & Structuring

A simple rule of thumb to calculate "Usable Equity" without paying LMI is: (Current Property Value x 0.80) minus Your Current Loan Balance. For example, if your home is worth $1,000,000 and you owe $600,000, your calculation is ($800,000 - $600,000) = $200,000 in usable equity. This $200k can be pulled out as a separate loan split to use as the 20% deposit and stamp duty for an investment purchase.

Cross-collateralisation occurs when a bank ties two or more properties together as security for one or multiple loans. It makes the bank very secure, but restricts you. If you want to sell one property or refinance, the bank controls the proceeds and may force you to pay down the remaining debt to meet their LVR requirements. We prefer to structure your loans as "standalone" facilities, giving you full control of your portfolio.

This depends on your accounting advice and cash-flow goals. Investors frequently use Interest-Only (IO) periods (usually 1-5 years) on investment properties to keep repayments low. They then direct all their spare cash to pay down their Principal Residence (non-tax-deductible debt) faster. Once the owner-occupied debt is cleared, they switch focus to the investment debt.

Self-Employed & Commercial Finance

It is not necessarily harder, but it requires more documentation. Major banks usually require 2 years of Company Tax Returns and NOAs, and they will often average the two years. However, if your business has seen a massive jump in profit this year, we have specialist lenders who will accept just 1 Year of financials, or even "Low-Doc" policies relying on BAS statements or Accountant declarations.

Commercial loans differ significantly. The maximum LVR is usually lower (often 65% to 75%, requiring a 25-35% deposit). The loan term is shorter (typically 15 to 25 years, not 30). Interest rates are generally higher, and lenders place massive weight on the strength of the lease, the yield, and the specific location/zoning of the asset (retail vs industrial warehouse vs office).

SMSF Lending (Self-Managed Super Funds)

Yes, your Self-Managed Super Fund (SMSF) can purchase residential property, but strict rules apply. The property must pass the "Sole Purpose Test" (providing retirement benefits). Crucially, you, your relatives, or related parties cannot live in the property, nor can you rent it from the fund. It must be an arm's-length investment.

An LRBA is the legally required structure for a super fund to borrow money. Because superannuation assets must be protected, the loan is "Limited Recourse." This means if the SMSF defaults on the property loan, the bank can only repossess the specific property held in the Bare Trust; they cannot touch the other cash, shares, or assets held inside the wider super fund.

If the property was purchased using borrowed money (an LRBA), you are allowed to repair and maintain the property, but you cannot fundamentally change the character of the asset (e.g., you cannot knock down a house and build a duplex, or turn a residential home into a commercial clinic) until the loan is fully paid off.
